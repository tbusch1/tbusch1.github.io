Gaming as a Hobby

- by Tom Busch -

About Me

My name is Tom Busch, and I have been gaming ever since I was around 8 years old. This hobby has always been a huge interest of mine, but it has grown and developed over the years. I have gone from playing the video game "Minecraft" on my dad's work computer to playing a wide array of games on a custom built gaming PC and other consoles. Throughout this time I have made great memories and met some awesome friends. I will continue to play video games well into the future and enjoy every second of it.

Home

The first video game was created in 1958 by a physicist named William Higinbotham. This game was similar to the widely known game Pong. It was played at the Brookhaven National Laboratory and given much praise. I am sure Higinbotham had no idea that his creation would lead to expansive and complex games like Read Dead Redemption 2 being made almost 70 years later.

Best Times

I have had so many amazing experiences playing video games throughout my life. I truly believe that these moments have made me who I am and given me some of the skills I have today. On top of this, I think playing video games and messing around with computer parts has contributed a lot towards my interest in computer science as a profession.

One of my fondest memories relating to video games and computers is when I built my first PC. This had been a goal for me since I began getting into playing video games. I started saving all the money I got from walking my neighbors dogs beginning in 5th grade, with the goal of spending it on computer parts. A few years later when I was in 8th grade, this hard work and saving paid off. I bought my parts, which were not half bad for the time (GTX 1060 6gb, i5 7400), and put it all together. To see something that I had saved for, bought with my own money, and built by hand, was a really amazing feeling. This computer has been upgraded and redone multiple times now, with only the hard drive and SSD remaining from the original build. This experience has taught me so much about saving money and working toward a goal.

Building my computer was a stand out memory to me and had a lot of influence on me growing up. As I have gotten older, gaming has given me countless other amazing memories. I met my best friend of about 7 years through gaming, when in 8th grade he asked me for advice on what games to play when his parents bought him a gaming laptop for christmas. We have bonded through video games over the years and our shared hobby has kept us in touch throughout our time at seperate highschools. I have also helped my girlfriend build her own gaming computer, allowing us to play games together. Our relationship has only gotten stronger because of the great gaming nights we have been able to share. Being able to see and interact with eachother in virtual spaces has even helped us through our current long distance situation.

Gaming as a hobby is so important to me simply because of the experiences that it has given me. I am forever thankful for what I have learned through gaming, and the relationships that it has helped me build.

Where

Gaming has been a huge part of my life in every place that I have lived. I started my journey playing Minecraft on my dad's computer in Austin, Texas at the age of around 8. I moved to California around that time and continued to explore my hobby. I met friends who introduced me to more games, and the concept of building computers. It was here that I began my goal of saving money and building my own computer. My hobby of gaming helped me to fit in and make new friends, as well as giving me an outlet for creativity and fun.

After moving to North Carolina a few years later, I met my best friend through a shared interest in video games. Our hobby has kept us close for many years and I am thankful for that relationship that was built so many years ago based on video games. Through any physical move or change in my life, video games have remained constant, and they will continue to do so. This has helped me cope through many things that life has thrown at me.

How

For someone with an interest of getting into playing video games, there are many options for places to start. You can look at buying a console like an XBOX, Playstation, or Nintendo Switch. You can also take the plunge and look at building or buying a gaming computer. The cost of any of these devices can be a hurdle to get into gaming, but the hours and hours of enjoyment you get from them makes it all worth it.

Why Play Video Games?

There is one very good reason to play video games... they're fun! Unlike some other hobbies, gaming won't necissarily make you more physically fit, however there are some other benefits. Gaming can improve your problem solving abilities, reaction time, and help make friends, either by people you meet in game or by improving social skills.

I wouldn't really recommend playing video games as a method of self improvement, however I believe that it can still have many benefits. As with anything, bad habits can absolutely be formed surrounding gaming. Everyone has heard of the stereotype of the seditary gamer wasting away their life in their room. I believe that this can easily be avoided if your relationship with video games are maintained in a healthy way. In the end, anything that is enjoyable is generally worth doing, as long as you are doing it in a healthy way.
